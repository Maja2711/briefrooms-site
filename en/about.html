About us

Why BriefRooms was created, how we work with sources, and the principles that guide the platform.

The BriefRooms mission

BriefRooms is not here to tell users what to think. It is here to provide organised information, credible sources, context and the tools needed to form their own view.

What is BriefRooms?

BriefRooms is an independent information and analysis platform that organises important developments and presents them in a clear, factual and concise format.

The project covers five main areas: News, Geopolitics, Health, Science and Investing. Our aim is not to publish the largest possible volume of content, but to select developments that genuinely matter, explain their context and identify their potential consequences.

We separate confirmed facts from interpretation, opinion, hypotheses and scenarios. Our materials identify publication and update dates, the sources used, and the role of artificial intelligence in preparing the content.

Quality and verification standards

Published materials undergo checks for source consistency, language quality and substantive accuracy. Information is compared with available data, primary documents and credible publications.

Where possible, we rely on primary sources, official institutional documents, market data, scientific publications and established news agencies. When the available evidence is incomplete, ambiguous or conflicting, we clearly state the level of uncertainty instead of presenting assumptions as confirmed information.

  • Source transparency
    Users should be able to understand the basis on which a material was prepared.
  • Separating facts from assessment
    Data, interpretation and scenarios are clearly distinguished.
  • Honest communication of uncertainty
    A lack of sufficient evidence is not replaced with false confidence.
  • Corrections and updates
    Content is corrected and updated when better data emerges or an error is demonstrated.

News and geopolitics

In News and Geopolitics, we focus on developments that may have social, economic, political or international significance. We do not stop at repeating the headline — we explain what happened, why it matters and what consequences may follow.

Reports from different publishers are compared and grouped around the underlying event. The same story is not presented as several separate items merely because it appeared in multiple outlets. When a situation is developing rapidly, the material is updated as new confirmed information becomes available.

Health and science

In Health and Science, published materials are grounded in confirmed data, credible sources, scientific publications and the current state of knowledge. The priority is to rely on evidence whose quality can be assessed through study design, sample size, reproducibility and consistency with the wider scientific record.

A single study, preliminary result or interesting hypothesis is not presented as a conclusive fact when it lacks sufficient confirmation from other evidence. Materials are updated when new, credible and corroborating evidence changes the current understanding.

A conclusion changes because of the quality and strength of the evidence, not because a view becomes popular, receives media attention or appears in one isolated publication. Medical materials are informational and educational and do not replace diagnosis, medical consultation or individually selected treatment.

At BriefRooms, the current state of knowledge is not treated as an unchangeable dogma, but changing it requires new, credible and corroborating evidence.

Investing and performance transparency

The Investing room presents market data, analysis of current developments, probabilistic scenarios, model-based weekly positions and a public model portfolio. For active positions, we show information including direction, entry price, current price, Stop Loss and Take Profit levels, potential reward-to-risk, current performance, position status and closed-position history.

The purpose is to show the full analytical process, not just the final result. Unsuccessful forecasts and losses remain in the history, and earlier assumptions are not rewritten after the fact to improve statistics. Analysis dates, entry and exit prices, risk levels, reasons for closure and methodology changes are preserved so performance can be assessed over a longer period.

The Daily Market Alert interprets important market moves after the US session opens and checks the picture again before the close. An update is published when price action, a key technical level, scenario probabilities or the main market driver changes materially. Scenarios are probabilistic assessments, not promises of a specific outcome.

A learning model

The BriefRooms investment model is being developed as a learning system that uses successive observations, results and the behaviour of different analytical methods. It compares strategy effectiveness across market environments and analyses signal quality, trend, momentum, volatility, entry and exit accuracy, and the relationship between returns and risk.

The system gradually adjusts the importance of individual methods according to their observed results. A strategy that performs well in a strong trend may not work equally well during consolidation or a sharp rise in volatility, so the model also accounts for changing market regimes.

The goal of the learning process is to achieve the highest possible effectiveness while maintaining risk control, transparent rules and a complete performance history. Methodology changes should follow from data and verifiable improvement, not from one successful or unsuccessful trade. The developing model does not guarantee profits and may still produce incorrect signals and losses.

The role of artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence supports data analysis, information organisation, source comparison, translation and the preparation of working drafts. It is not treated as an independent source of facts.

AI-generated or AI-assisted content is subject to the same verification principles as other materials. Information is published on the basis of available sources and data, not solely because it was generated by a language model.

Independence, responsibility and corrections

BriefRooms is an information and analysis project. Published materials do not constitute personalised investment recommendations or financial, legal or medical advice. Users should assess the information independently and consult an appropriate professional when necessary.

Commercial cooperation, partnerships and brand references do not influence the substance of analyses or the results of the models presented. Sponsored materials, where present, are clearly labelled.

Any user may report an error, an inaccuracy or a missing source. Material corrections are introduced with an update notice. Our objective is not to defend a published conclusion at all costs, but to correct it when better evidence appears or an error is demonstrated.
