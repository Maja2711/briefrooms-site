About BriefRooms

A short explanation of how BriefRooms works, which sources we rely on, and what you can use it for.

BriefRooms was created for people who want to stay informed but do not have time to read dozens of long articles every day. Instead of flooding you with information, we pick the most relevant topics and turn them into short, practical summaries.

The platform is organised into four rooms: News, Geopolitics, Health and Science. In each room you will find selected materials from reliable sources, written in clear language and focused on what really matters: key claims, main takeaways and potential consequences.

Sources, research and the search for truth

Our goal is to rely only on trusted sources and research, so that we can move step by step towards the truth, regardless of what that truth turns out to be. Whenever robust data contradict popular narratives, we prioritise evidence and methodology over convenient stories.

In practice this means that we try to:

  • base our main claims on solid studies, reports or sources with a known level of reliability,
  • clearly separate what follows directly from the data from commentary, interpretation and hypotheses,
  • highlight areas where scientific knowledge is limited or where studies give conflicting results.

AI in the background, humans in control

In the background we use AI (artificial intelligence) to search sources and generate first drafts of briefs. Every summary is then reviewed and edited by a human to keep it concise, understandable and as unbiased as possible.

How should you use BriefRooms?

We are not a traditional news portal, nor do we provide financial or medical advice. Think of BriefRooms as a starting point: a quick overview of the key information that helps you decide what is worth exploring further in the original, specialist sources.
