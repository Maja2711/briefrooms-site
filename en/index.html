France passes law banning under-15s from social media

The law will mean everyone in France must verify their age to access social media. From September, people under 15 will not be able to open accounts, and age verification will be required on all new accounts. French Digital Minister Anne Le Hénanff defended the speed of the law's implementation, stating that age-verification tools already exist. France is the second country to introduce a ban on underage social media use, following Australia.

More young women in their 20s are getting type 2 diabetes

The rise in type 2 diabetes among young women in their 20s has been linked to increasing obesity levels since the Covid pandemic. The disease can lead to severe complications, and early diagnosis is crucial to prevent long-term organ damage. Researchers suggest that newer obesity treatments may help manage the condition alongside lifestyle changes.

NISAR’s L-Band Radar Reveals ‘Hummingbird’ in Antarctica

The NISAR satellite's L-band radar has produced an image of Nunatak Zaterjavshijsja in East Antarctica. This mountaintop is surrounded by a stream of ice, causing stresses that lead to deep cracks known as crevasses. The image, nicknamed 'the hummingbird,' was created using polarized microwave signals. NISAR is the first satellite to carry two SAR instruments at different wavelengths.
