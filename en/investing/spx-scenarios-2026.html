Geopolitical filter

The geopolitical filter does not reverse the current positive base bias, but it lowers conviction to 6/10. It acts as a risk-control layer across all horizons, especially 1M and 12M.

  • Middle East and oil: escalation in the region can lift energy prices, revive inflation pressure and weaken sentiment toward risk assets.
  • U.S.-China: trade, technology and sanctions are risks for margins, supply chains, semiconductors and the AI capex narrative.
  • Eastern Europe: prolonged tensions increase the risk premium in Europe, can affect energy and may strengthen demand for the dollar.
  • Inflation: an energy or logistics shock makes easier Fed communication harder, with the strongest impact on the 1M horizon.
  • Dollar: global risk-off usually supports USD, which can tighten financial conditions for some markets and international companies.
  • Sentiment and bond yields: geopolitics can lift VIX while also moving bond yields if investors price an inflationary shock.

What changed since 13 June 2026

  • 1M: mildly positive → neutral. The combination of 10Y yields, VIX and softer payrolls does not provide a sufficient directional edge.
  • 6M: positive → unchanged. Early earnings support strong EPS growth and NFCI remains below zero.
  • 12M: mildly positive → unchanged. Fundamentals are sound, but valuation and concentration require caution.
  • Conviction: 6/10 → 6/10. Disinflation and earnings offset weaker labour data and geopolitics.
  • Added an evidence ledger, contradiction check, thesis clock and two-confirmation rule.

What worked / what we are learning

Worked: 6M remained the strongest horizon; earnings and AI capex still dominate, FOMC did not trigger the invalidation gate and NFCI stayed below zero.

Model correction: the previous 1M view was too one-sided. The model now separates fundamentals from the tactical volatility regime.

Thesis clock: month 1 of 6. We do not judge a 6M thesis on one week; the next test is whether earnings strength broadens beyond the largest contributors.

Methodology — aligned with Investments 10k

  1. Evidence ledger: observation, source, date, direction, horizon and limitation.
  2. Score ≠ confidence: bias describes direction; conviction describes evidence quality.
  3. Contradiction check: opposing evidence is published explicitly.
  4. Regime gate: a change requires two independent confirmations.
  5. Thesis clock: each thesis is judged on its stated horizon.
  6. No silent rewrite: every version discloses the bias change and its cause.

Monthly review, or an exceptional review after VIX ≥30, NFCI >0, 10Y >5%, or simultaneous deterioration in EPS and labour data.
