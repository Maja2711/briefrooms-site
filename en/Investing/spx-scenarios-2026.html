Intro ‚Äî what this text is (and is not)

This article summarises three base-case scenarios for the S&P 500 index over roughly 1 month, 6 months and 12 months. It is anchored in IMF and OECD forecasts for global growth, equity valuations, monetary policy paths for the Fed/ECB, and the latest public research from large investment banks.

The current consensus of international institutions is that in 2024‚Äì2026 the world economy will grow by around 3‚Äì3.2% per year, below the long-term average, with inflation gradually returning towards central-bank targets. The IMF‚Äôs World Economic Outlook (Oct 2025) projects global GDP growth of about 3.3% in 2024, 3.2% in 2025 and 3.1% in 2026, with advanced economies growing near 1.5%. The OECD‚Äôs Economic Outlook paints a very similar picture: steady but modest growth and a slow disinflation process.

On top of this macro backdrop, investment banks build their equity scenarios. Goldman Sachs and others stress that with such growth and inflation, risk assets can still have tailwinds, but the distribution of outcomes is wider than a few years ago (geopolitics, fiscal policy, elections, trade tensions). Evercore ISI openly uses a ‚Äúbig beautiful bubble‚Äù narrative for US equities, with a base case for the S&P 500 near 7750 by end-2026 and a non-zero probability path towards 9000 if AI-driven earnings growth and risk appetite persist.

All levels and ranges in this text are illustrative and hypothetical. This is an educational overview ‚Äî not investment advice and not a recommendation to buy or sell any instrument.

1. 1M horizon ‚Äî tactical scenario and liquidity

Momentum, CTAs and short-term volatility

In the very short run, markets are dominated by: labour-market data, short-term rates (SOFR), systematic/CTA positioning, and flows into trend-following strategies. When a long streak of trading above the 50-day moving average finally breaks, many CTA models mechanically reduce long exposure, which can amplify short but sharp pullbacks in the order of several percent.

SOFR, the TGA and the plumbing of the system

Since the pandemic, investors have learned to watch not only the level of policy rates, but the liquidity plumbing: the short-term funding rate (SOFR) and the Treasury General Account (TGA) balance at the Fed influence the amount of bank reserves and financial conditions. A rapid rebuild of the TGA after debt-ceiling episodes typically means a temporary liquidity drain, which can produce an ‚Äúair pocket‚Äù in risk assets ‚Äî sudden drops even if the macro data have not changed dramatically.

Base case (1M)

Taking into account:

  • still relatively low near-term recession risk in the US in IMF/OECD assessments,
  • expectations that the Fed‚Äôs first meaningful rate cuts arrive more in 2026 than immediately,
  • CTA positioning after an extended up-trend,

the base-case 1M scenario is: a correction and elevated volatility within an ongoing bull market. We can expect back-and-forth moves triggered by macro releases and Fed communication, but no clear signal yet that the cycle is over.

In practice, this means that in such a short horizon risk management (position sizing, leverage, stop-loss levels) matters more than trying to catch every small swing.

2. 6M horizon ‚Äî to mid-2026 (macro, AI and end of tightening)

Macro: moderate but positive growth

On a six-month horizon, the macro environment dominates. The IMF‚Äôs WEO 2025 and the OECD‚Äôs Outlook both suggest a world that is not in recession, but not booming either: steady growth, slightly below pre-pandemic averages, and inflation slowly moving towards target ranges.

For equities, the key ingredients are:

  • inflation drifting closer to central-bank targets,
  • no sudden spike in unemployment,
  • a gradual move of policy rates from ‚Äúrestrictive‚Äù towards ‚Äúneutral‚Äù territory as the Fed and ECB finish their hiking cycles and start cautious cuts.

With that backdrop, most large-bank 6‚Äì12M outlooks remain constructive for risk assets, but with a strong caveat: valuations are elevated, and the market has become more discriminating across sectors and single names.

AI, capex and corporate earnings

Many houses (Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, UBS, JPMorgan and others) expect investment in AI, automation and energy infrastructure to be a key driver of S&P 500 earnings in the coming years. In their base cases, index-level earnings grow at a mid-single- to low-double-digit pace, which, assuming stable margins and no recession, can support indices near or above previous highs.

QT, the TGA and bank reserves

From a liquidity perspective, a move from aggressive quantitative tightening (QT) towards a more neutral Fed balance sheet, together with a stabilised TGA balance, usually supports easier financial conditions: credit spreads are less volatile and the market absorbs Treasury issuance more smoothly.

Base case (6M)

The 6M base case can be summarised as:

  • one or more 8‚Äì12% corrections along the way,
  • no hard landing in the US economy,
  • the ‚Äúsoft-landing / no-landing‚Äù narrative remaining the dominant one,
  • the S&P 500 revisiting or slightly exceeding prior highs if earnings deliver against expectations.

This is still an environment where ‚Äúbuying the dip‚Äù can work, but no longer ‚Äúbuy anything at any price‚Äù. Sector and quality selection (balance sheet strength, cash-flow generation, real AI projects instead of pure stories) becomes critical.

3. 12M horizon ‚Äî to end-2026 (late bull and bubble tail risk)

Global picture: the ‚Äútepid twenties‚Äù

IMF and OECD economists increasingly talk about ‚Äútepid twenties‚Äù ‚Äî a decade of cool-ish growth with recurring shocks (wars, trade frictions, energy transition), rather than a return to the pre-2008 boom. IMF projections point to global growth around 3.3% in 2024, 3.2% in 2025 and 3.1% in 2026; the OECD‚Äôs Interim Outlook suggests similar numbers, even dipping towards ~2.9% in some scenarios.

For equities, this implies that the long-term ‚Äúgrowth from the real economy‚Äù is modest. For the S&P 500 to grow faster, we need a combination of:

  • earnings growth,
  • multiple expansion (higher P/E),
  • and, in the more extreme path, outright over-paying for AI-related stories.

Valuations, AI and the ‚Äúbig beautiful bubble‚Äù

Analysts at Evercore ISI, Morgan Stanley, Schroders and others highlight several facts:

  • on most metrics (P/E, CAPE, yield gap vs Treasuries), S&P 500 valuations are above long-term averages,
  • market cap is extremely concentrated in a dozen or so mega-cap tech/AI names,
  • retail and passive flows increasingly buy the index regardless of valuation levels.

Evercore ISI‚Äôs base case sees the S&P 500 near 7750 by end-2026, but their ‚Äúbubble scenario‚Äù stretches towards 9000 if AI-related earnings, lower rates and risk appetite all line up favourably. The probability of that path is limited but not zero.

Base case (12M)

Given today‚Äôs macro data and analyst consensus, the 12M base case looks roughly like this:

  • the S&P 500 has a non-zero chance of ending 2026 higher than today (positive nominal returns),
  • real returns will be lower because inflation eats part of those nominal gains,
  • at least one 15‚Äì25% drawdown along the way is very plausible, driven by macro or geopolitical surprise (trade wars, elections, conflict in Asia, etc.),
  • the further the index moves above base-case targets (say, well beyond 7400‚Äì7600), the more it makes sense to reduce risk at the margin rather than increase it.

This is not a classic ‚Äúcrash call‚Äù, but rather an emphasis on asymmetry: there is upside left, but each extra +10% in price is bought with a worse reward-to-risk profile.

Buy & sell zones ‚Äî 1M / 6M / 12M

The following buy and sell zones are indicative only and aligned with the base scenarios above. They are not ‚Äúhard signals‚Äù, but ranges where the reward-to-risk trade-off typically looks better (buy zones) or worse (sell / de-risk zones) in a late-cycle bull market.

As a reference point we use the S&P 500 level around 6617 (19 Nov 2025) and historically typical late-cycle pullbacks: a few percent in the very short term, around 8‚Äì12% over a half-year window and up to 15‚Äì25% in a 12-month window without a full-blown recession.

1M ‚Äî tactical levels

  • Potential buy zone (1M): 6350‚Äì6450 (roughly 3‚Äì4% below the reference level).
  • Potential sell / take-profit zone (1M): 6850‚Äì6950 (roughly 3‚Äì5% above the reference level).

Rationale: in the very short term, price action is driven mainly by technicals (30‚Äì60 day moving averages), CTA/trend-follower flows and liquidity shifts (SOFR, TGA). A 3‚Äì4% pullback often brings the index back towards shorter moving averages where, in the absence of bad macro news, institutional demand tends to re-appear. Conversely, a quick 3‚Äì5% breakout above recent highs without a fundamental catalyst frequently triggers short-term profit-taking.

6M ‚Äî half-year cycle

  • Potential buy zone (6M): 5900‚Äì6100 (about 8‚Äì12% below the reference level).
  • Potential sell / de-risk zone (6M): 7100‚Äì7300 (around 7‚Äì10% above the reference level).

Rationale: on a six-month horizon, late-cycle bull markets often see 10% pullbacks that drive the index below longer moving averages (MA60‚Äì100) but do not break the uptrend ‚Äî provided that: growth and labour data remain reasonable and central banks are moving from tightening towards neutral or modest easing. From a valuation standpoint, the 7100‚Äì7300 area implies clearly higher multiples relative to bond yields; the reward-to-risk for adding new long exposure becomes less attractive there.

12M ‚Äî strategic levels towards end-2026

  • Long-term buy zone (12M): 5500‚Äì5700 (about 13‚Äì17% below the reference level).
  • Very high valuation / structural de-risk zone (12M): 7400‚Äì7600 (around 12‚Äì15% above the reference level).

Rationale: over a full year, the probability of a deeper ‚Äúflush-out‚Äù rises ‚Äî 15‚Äì25% drawdowns have occurred many times in history without a Great-Recession-type event (examples: 2011, 2015‚Äì2016, 2018). The 5500‚Äì5700 zone corresponds to such a cyclical correction but not yet to a deep secular bear market. On the other side, the 7400‚Äì7600 zone is broadly in line with major banks‚Äô base-case targets for end-2026; beyond that area, the market would be trading in an ‚Äúextremely rich valuation‚Äù regime where any negative macro, policy or regulatory surprise could trigger sharp profit-taking.

Important: these ranges should not be treated as automatic trade signals. They are an orientation map to help think about the scale of corrections and upside potential in the context of a late-cycle bull market, liquidity (SOFR, TGA), valuations and consensus research.
