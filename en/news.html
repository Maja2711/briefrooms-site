  • Space jam: astronomers detect ‘raspberry sugar’ on dust cloud in Milky Way
    Source: The Guardian
    Comment
    Astronomers have detected the simple sugar erythrulose in a dust cloud near the center of the Milky Way. This discovery suggests that compounds essential for life can form in interstellar space. Erythrulose is produced through chemical reactions on tiny dust grains and may rain down on planets or be delivered by comets. This finding is significant as it is the first detection of sugar in interstellar space, indicating that such compounds may be more common than previously thought.
